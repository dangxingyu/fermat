\begin{proof}
Suppose for contradiction that there are only finitely many primes, say $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n$. Consider the number
\[
N = p_1 p_2 \cdots p_n + 1.
\]
Since $N > 1$, it has at least one prime divisor $p$. But for each $p_i$, we have $p_i \mid p_1 p_2 \cdots p_n$, so $p_i \nmid N$. Thus $p$ is a prime not in our list, a contradiction. Hence there are infinitely many primes.
\end{proof}
