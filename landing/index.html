Why Fermat

Everything you need to prove theorems faster.

✏️

Monaco LaTeX editor

Full syntax highlighting, auto-completion, and auto-closing environments — the VS Code editing experience inside your theorem prover.

🤖

3-phase proof pipeline

Sketch → Prove → Verify. Claude plans the approach, writes a rigorous \begin{proof}, then acts as judge — retrying once if it fails.

🔍

Context-aware proving

The engine reads your entire document — definitions, prior lemmas, the dependency graph — so proofs reference your actual theorems, not generic placeholders.

📋

Review before inserting

Easy proofs auto-inline. Medium and Hard proofs land in the Review Panel so you can read, edit, accept, or reject before they touch your document.

🔗

SyncTeX navigation

Click in the PDF → jump to the source line. Press the sync button → PDF scrolls to your cursor. Forward and inverse search, out of the box.

📦

Ships as a desktop app

Electron-packaged for macOS (Universal), Windows, and Linux. No web account needed — your documents and API key stay local.

How it works

One comment triggers the whole pipeline.

\begin{theorem}[Infinitude of Primes] \label{thm:inf-primes} There are infinitely many prime numbers. \end{theorem} % [PROVE IT: Easy] ← Fermat sees this, queues a proof task \begin{theorem}[Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic] \label{thm:fta} Every integer $n > 1$ has a unique prime factorisation. \end{theorem} % [PROVE IT: Hard] ← sketch → prove → verify pipeline runs % SKETCH: Two parts — existence by induction, uniqueness via Euclid's lemma.

Click Submit All in the toolbar. Easy proofs appear instantly inline. Hard proofs land in the Review Panel where you edit and accept them before they're inserted — keeping you in full control of your document.
